PART 4 · ALERTS & DECISION LOGIC [BUILDOTS+]
4

Alerts & Decision Logic

What to act on now — triaged by consequence, not a flat list.

  • 🔴 Critical — ~24–48h; client or Readiness SLA breach.
  • 🟠 At risk — this week, you own the timing.
  • 🟢 Monitor — awareness only, ambient digest.
  • ⚪ Snoozed — parked; waiting or no resource now.
The 5 triggers → action
#TriggerAction
1Readiness gap inside the 10-day buffer, no Ready techredeploy a returning tech / escalate
2Acceleration breaches lead timerecompute runway; cover or escalate
3No-show / cancel at deploypool reassignment; auto-bench
4Recruitment window opens, no techstart recruiting / reserve a pool tech
5Coverage shortfall < 90%attribute cause before penalising
Logic

Alerts fire automatically; you can also add one manually.

Go deeper, per project

[ OPERATING-LOGIC ASSUMPTIONS ] [BUILDOTS+]

Key assumptions

SLA
Projects delivered on or before the committed date. For simplicity, this excludes other agreement components such as return visits and additional terms.
Readiness SLA
Ready ≥ 10 business days before Requested Delivery. Internal; the leading indicator of the Account SLA.
Utilization over headcount
Size crews to keep technicians and vendors utilized, not to grow headcount. Bounded by vendor contract terms (minimums, commitments) and by geography — a technician serves same-region work; cross-region travel is costly.
Two deploy paths
New-hire (~6-wk spine) vs returning (days; skips Training + PPE). KPI = % returning deployments.
Source of truth
Jira + Confluence; the mockup = the ideal Jira for this operation. Channels = provided mix (Craigslist-dominant); vendor (Cloud Factory) = strategic direction.
